# Template: Corporate (Corporate Documents) — English

## Use This Template For
- Board resolutions
- Shareholder or director minutes
- Articles / bylaws / internal governance instruments
- Powers of attorney / proxies
- Internal corporate regulations and governance policies

## Support Profile
- Support level: `Full`
- No authority packet is required for baseline drafting
- Entity facts, meeting details, governance structure, and requested decisions must still come from the user or source materials

## Section Tags
- `[B]` = Standard framing language or customary section
- `[S]` = Entity-specific governance or decision content

## Variant A — Board Resolution

1. `BOARD RESOLUTIONS OF [COMPANY NAME]` `[B]`
2. `Background / Recitals` `[S]`
   - Use `WHEREAS` clauses for US-style formal resolutions when appropriate
   - Use plainer recital paragraphs if the selected house style is UK or international
3. `Resolutions` `[S]`
   - `RESOLVED, that [resolution text]`
   - `FURTHER RESOLVED, that [follow-on authority or implementation step]`
   - Include effective date, authority to sign, and filing or notice steps if needed
4. `Implementation / Officer Authority` `[S]`
5. `Certification` `[B/S]`
   - Secretary or authorized officer certification
6. `Date and Signatures` `[B/S]`

## Boilerplate vs Substantive Guidance
- Boilerplate: titles, certification language, approval language, amendment clauses, signature blocks
- Substantive: recital facts, adopted resolutions, voting outcomes, governance allocations, delegated authority
- Avoid vague resolutions such as `management shall proceed as appropriate`; specify who is authorized to do what and when

## Drafting Reminders
- Use the convention layer to decide whether headings should be all caps, title case, or sentence case
- For minutes, distinguish clearly between discussion summary and formal action taken
- For constitutive documents, maintain stable numbering so downstream revisions can preserve clause identity
